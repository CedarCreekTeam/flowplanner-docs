# Getting started guide

This is your step-by-step path from a brand-new account to a live, collecting-feedback board. You can be up and running in **5 minutes**.

> [!quote] From zero to feedback in minutes
> FlowPlanner is built to ==get out of your way==. Set it up once, share the link, and let your users start talking.

## Quick start (5 min)

### Step 1: Create your account (1 min)

- Go to **[flowplanner.io](https://flowplanner.io)** and click **Start for free**
- Enter your email and password, then verify your email
- [[Creating Your Account|Full account setup guide →]]

### Step 2: Set up your organization (2 min)

- Enter your name, your **board name**, and choose a **subdomain**
- Your board will live at `yoursubdomain.flowplanner.io`
- [[Setting Up Your First Board|Full board setup guide →]]

### Step 3: Create your categories (1 min)

Categories help organize feedback into buckets your team can act on.

- Go to **Admin → Categories**
- Create 3--5 categories to start (e.g., "Feature Request", "Bug", "Improvement", "Question")
- [[Categories|Learn more about categories →]]

### Step 4: Share your board (1 min)

- Copy your board URL: `yoursubdomain.flowplanner.io`
- Share it in your app, docs, email footer, or social channels
- Your first feedback will arrive before you know it

---

## Complete setup (15 min)

Ready to build a proper system? This covers everything you'll want before announcing your board publicly.

### Configure board settings

- Set whether users must be **signed in** to submit feedback or vote
- Choose whether your board is **public** or **invite-only**
- Add a **description** to tell visitors what your board is for
- [[Board Settings|Full settings guide →]]

### Invite your team

Bring in co-founders, PMs, or support teammates so everyone can help triage.

- Go to **Admin → Team**
- Click **Invite member** and enter their email
- [[Inviting Team Members|Learn about roles and inviting →]]

### Review your first posts

Once feedback starts coming in, your workflow will be:

1. A user submits a post
2. Your team reviews it in **Admin → Feedback**
3. Set a **status** and **category** to keep things organized
4. When you're ready to act on it, move it to **Planned** or **In Progress** -- it appears on your roadmap automatically

---

## Pro tips for your first week

> [!tip] Don't overthink categories
> Start with 3--4 broad categories. You can always add more later. Too many categories upfront makes it harder for users to choose the right one.

> [!tip] Announce your board to real users
> Don't just open it to the world quietly. Email your current users with a direct link and ask them to share what they want most. A few real posts seed the board better than dozens of test entries.

> [!success] Closing the loop is the secret
> The biggest thing that makes a feedback board ==feel alive== is marking things "Released" when you ship them. Users who see their request go from Open → Released become your biggest advocates.
