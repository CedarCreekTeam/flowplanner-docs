# What is FlowPlanner?

FlowPlanner is a **public feedback and roadmap tool** for product teams. Give your users a place to submit ideas, vote on what matters most, and follow along as you build.

> [!quote] The FlowPlanner idea
> Your best product decisions come from your users. FlowPlanner gives them ==a direct line to your roadmap== -- and keeps them engaged while you build.

## What it does

FlowPlanner has three core pieces that work together:

**1. A public feedback board**
Your users visit your board, submit feedback, and vote on posts from other users. Every vote is a signal -- the most-wanted features rise to the top automatically.

**2. A workflow for your team**
Your admin team triages incoming feedback, sets statuses, responds to posts, and links related ideas. You always know what's been reviewed and what's waiting.

**3. A public roadmap**
As you update statuses -- marking posts as Planned, In Progress, or Released -- your roadmap updates automatically. Users can see what you're working on without you writing a single update.

## Why teams use FlowPlanner

> [!success] FlowPlanner is designed for...
> - SaaS teams who want structured feedback instead of scattered emails and Slack threads
> - Product managers who need to prioritize features based on real demand
> - Teams who want to ==keep users in the loop== without a newsletter or changelog
> - Founders who want to show users their voice is heard

## Key concepts

| Term | What it means |
|------|--------------|
| **Board** | Your public feedback page, hosted at `yourname.flowplanner.io` |
| **Post** | A piece of feedback submitted by a user |
| **Vote** | A user endorsing a post they want to see built |
| **Status** | Where a post is in your workflow: Open, Planned, In Progress, Released, or Closed |
| **Category** | A label that groups similar posts (e.g., "Feature Request", "Bug", "Improvement") |
| **Roadmap** | The public view of all posts grouped by status |

---

## What FlowPlanner is not

**Not a support tool.** FlowPlanner is for collecting feature feedback and showing your roadmap -- not for handling support tickets or bug reports with SLA tracking. Use it alongside your support system.

**Not a project management tool.** FlowPlanner doesn't replace Linear, Jira, or your internal task tracker. It captures the *signal* from users -- you decide what to build.

**Not enterprise software.** FlowPlanner is deliberately simple and affordable. No complex setup, no per-seat pricing at scale, no sales calls required.
