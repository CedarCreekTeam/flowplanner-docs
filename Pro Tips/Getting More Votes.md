# Getting more votes

A board with votes is a useful prioritization tool. A board with no votes is just a list of text. Here's how to drive real engagement from your actual users.

> [!quote] Votes are a lagging indicator of visibility
> If your board isn't getting votes, it's not a sign that users don't have opinions -- it's a sign they ==don't know your board exists==.

## Make your board impossible to miss

The most common reason a board gets no traffic: users don't know it exists. Fix this before anything else.

> [!success] High-visibility placements
> - **In your app** -- a "Give feedback" or "Request a feature" link in the main navigation or footer
> - **After key moments** -- show a link to your board after a user completes a core action ("Enjoying this? Tell us what to add next →")
> - **In error messages** -- "Something's not working? Let us know →"
> - **In your onboarding** -- introduce the board as "your direct line to our roadmap"

---

## Seed the board with real posts

An empty board gets no votes. Seed it by submitting posts yourself based on feedback you've already heard:

1. Think of the 5 most common feature requests you've gotten via email, support, or Slack
2. Submit them as posts (you can submit on behalf of users if they gave you the idea)
3. These posts will attract real votes because they represent real demand

> [!tip] Phrase posts from the user's perspective
> "Add dark mode" (user request) gets more votes than "Implement theme switching" (technical framing). Write titles the way users would.

---

## Ask users to vote, not just submit

Voting on an existing post is a ==lower-friction action== than submitting a new one. When a user reaches out with a request, respond with: "Great idea -- I've added it to our feedback board. You can vote on it here: [link]."

---

## Close the loop publicly

> [!success] The virtuous cycle
> 1. User votes → feels heard
> 2. Post moves to Planned → user checks back
> 3. Post ships → user shares your roadmap
> 4. New users discover your board via the share
> 5. New users vote → repeat

Every Released post you announce is an invitation for more users to engage with your board.

---

## Avoid gaming your own board

> [!note] Don't manufacture votes
> Vote counts are only useful if they reflect genuine user demand. Don't ask employees to vote on posts artificially, don't run contests that incentivize voting, and don't create fake accounts. You're optimizing for the signal -- ==polluting it defeats the purpose==.
