# Roadmap overview

Your roadmap is a ==living, public view of what you're building== -- automatically generated from the statuses you set on feedback posts. No separate tool, no manual updates, no changelog to maintain.

> [!quote] Transparency builds trust
> Users who can see your roadmap don't send "any update on this?" emails. They check your board, see the progress, and ==feel connected to what you're building==.

## How the roadmap works

The roadmap pulls posts directly from your feedback board based on their status:

- **Planned** → appears in the Planned column
- **In Progress** → appears in the In Progress column
- **Released** → appears in the Released column

Posts with Open or Closed status do not appear by default. You can also manually pin any post to the Planned column using the **Add to roadmap** toggle in the post detail sidebar -- even if its status hasn't changed.

There's nothing to configure -- the roadmap updates the moment you change a status or toggle roadmap visibility.

---

## Navigating the roadmap

Visitors click the **Roadmap** tab on your board to see:

- Three columns (Planned, In Progress, Released) with post cards in each
- Each card shows the post title, vote count, and category
- Clicking a card opens the full post with its description and comments

---

## Keeping your roadmap honest

The roadmap is only as useful as the accuracy of your statuses.

> [!note] Roadmap health checks
> - **Planned column too full?** You may have over-committed. Review and close posts that aren't genuinely on your near-term roadmap.
> - **In Progress for too long?** Consider adding a comment with a timeline or expected ship date.
> - **Released column empty?** Make sure you're marking posts Released when you ship -- even small improvements count.

---

## What makes a great roadmap

> [!success] Signs of a healthy roadmap
> - **Released column has entries** -- your users can see you're actually shipping
> - **In Progress is short** -- 1--3 items, not 20
> - **Planned is realistic** -- things you'll genuinely build in the next few months
> - **Posts have comments** -- users get context, not just a status label
