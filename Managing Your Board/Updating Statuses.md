# Updating statuses

Updating a post's status is your most powerful tool for communicating with users. It moves posts through your workflow, drives the public roadmap, and closes the feedback loop when you ship.

> [!quote] Every status change is a message to your users
> You don't have to write a blog post or send a newsletter. Marking a post ==Released== tells every voter "we heard you, and we built it."

## How to update a status

**From the admin list:**
1. Go to **Admin → Feedback**
2. Click on a post to open it
3. Find the **Status** dropdown
4. Select the new status
5. The change is saved and reflected immediately on the public board

---

## When to use each status

### Open → Planned

Use this when you've made a real, committed decision to build something. Don't mark things Planned speculatively. ==Planned is a promise.==

> [!note] Be deliberate with Planned
> Users watch the Planned column. If you mark something Planned and it sits there for 6 months, trust erodes. Only use Planned when it's genuinely in your near-term roadmap.

### Planned → In Progress

When your team starts actively working on the feature. This is optional but signals momentum.

### In Progress → Released

When the feature ships to users. This is the ==most important status update== you can make. Mark things Released promptly after shipping.

> [!success] The released moment
> Users who see their request go Released become advocates. They share the product, they renew subscriptions, and they submit more high-quality feedback. Never skip marking things Released.

### Any status → Closed

When you've decided not to build something, it's a duplicate, or it's out of scope. Always leave a comment explaining why.

---

## Bulk status updates

In the admin feedback list, you can select multiple posts and update their status in one action:

1. Check the checkbox next to each post you want to update
2. A bulk action bar appears at the bottom of the screen
3. Use the **Set status** dropdown to pick the new status
4. Click **Apply**

> [!tip] Bulk-close old Open posts periodically
> If you have posts from a year ago that are still Open and will never be built, batch-closing them with a note ("Closing older posts that aren't on our current roadmap -- please resubmit if this is still relevant to you") keeps your board clean and current.
