# Managing categories

Categories help users classify their feedback and help your team filter and triage it. Good category design makes your board easier to use for everyone.

> [!quote] Categories are your first triage layer
> The right categories mean you can open your admin panel and ==immediately know which posts to route to which team member==.

## Creating a category

1. Go to **Admin → Categories**
2. Click **Add category**
3. Enter a **name** (keep it short -- users see this in a dropdown)
4. Choose a **color** for visual distinction
5. Click **Save**

The category appears immediately in the submit form on your public board.

---

## Editing a category

1. Go to **Admin → Categories**
2. Click the **edit** icon next to the category name
3. Change the name or color
4. Save

> [!note] Renaming a category
> When you rename a category, existing posts with that category are ==updated immediately==. The new name applies retroactively.

---

## Deleting a category

1. Go to **Admin → Categories**
2. Click the **delete** icon next to a category
3. Confirm the deletion

> [!note] What happens to posts with a deleted category
> Existing posts that used the deleted category keep their category label in the admin view. They won't be re-categorized automatically -- you'll need to update them manually if the category was heavily used.

---

## Reordering categories

Drag categories in the **Admin → Categories** list to reorder them. The order here matches the order users see in the submit form and the filter bar.

> [!tip] Put your most common category first
> The first category in your list is the default selection in the submit form. Put your most common type (usually "Feature Request") first to reduce friction for users.

---

## How many categories is right?

> [!success] The 5-rule of thumb
> Most boards work best with **5--7 categories**. Enough to route different types of feedback to different team workflows, few enough that users don't have to think hard about which one to pick.

Common patterns:
- **3 categories:** Feature, Bug, Other
- **5 categories:** Feature Request, Bug, Improvement, Question, Other
- **7 categories:** Break "Feature" into subcategories that match your product areas
