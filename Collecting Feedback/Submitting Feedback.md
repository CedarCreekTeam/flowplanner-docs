# Submitting feedback

> [!note] This article is written for end users visiting a FlowPlanner-powered board.

Submitting feedback is how you tell a product team what you want. Your post is visible to all visitors, and other users can vote on it to signal they want the same thing.

> [!quote] The more specific, the more useful
> A post that says "better search" is hard to act on. A post that says "search should filter by date range" is ==immediately actionable==.

## Before you submit

**Search first.** Someone may have already submitted the same idea. Voting on an existing post is more powerful than creating a duplicate -- it combines everyone's signal into one high-visibility request.

1. Use the **search bar** at the top of the board
2. Browse the **category filters** to find related posts
3. If you find your idea, click **Vote** on it instead of submitting a new one

---

## How to submit a post

1. Click **Create a post** (or the `+` button) on the feedback board
2. Enter a **title** -- keep it short and specific (e.g., "Add CSV export for all contacts")
3. Add a **description** -- explain the problem you're solving or the context that makes this valuable
4. Choose a **category** -- pick the type that fits best (Feature Request, Bug, Improvement, etc.)
5. Click **Submit**

Your post appears immediately on the board.

---

## Writing a good post

> [!tip] What makes a post get traction
> - **Specific title** -- describe the feature or problem precisely
> - **Context in the description** -- explain *why* you need this, not just what you want
> - **Real use case** -- "I need this because..." is far more compelling than "It would be nice if..."

> [!success] Example of a strong post
> **Title:** "Export feedback list to CSV"
> **Description:** "I'm trying to share our top feature requests with stakeholders in a board meeting. A CSV export from the admin panel would save me 20 minutes of copy-pasting every time."

---

## After submitting

Your post is live immediately. Other users can vote on it and comment. The product team will review it and may:

- Set a status (Planned, In Progress, etc.)
- Respond with a comment
- Close it with an explanation if it's not a fit

You'll receive email notifications when the status changes if you're signed in.
