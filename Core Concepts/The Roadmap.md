# The roadmap

Your roadmap is a ==live, automatic view of what you're building== -- organized by the status of each post. No manual updates, no changelog to maintain. When you move a post to Planned, it's on the roadmap. When you ship it and mark it Released, it's there too.

> [!quote] Your roadmap updates itself
> Every status change you make in the admin panel is ==immediately reflected== on your public roadmap. Your users always see the current picture.

## What the roadmap shows

The roadmap organizes posts into three columns:

| Column | What appears here |
|--------|-------------------|
| **Planned** | Posts you've committed to building |
| **In Progress** | Posts actively being worked on |
| **Released** | Posts that have shipped |

Posts with status Open or Closed do not appear on the roadmap.

---

## Why a public roadmap matters

> [!success] What users feel when they see your roadmap
> - Their feedback was heard
> - You're actively building
> - It's worth voting and checking back

A public roadmap turns passive users into ==engaged advocates==. Users who see their request move from Open → Planned → Released are far more likely to stay subscribed, refer others, and keep voting.

---

## How to populate your roadmap

You don't need to do anything special. Just update the status of existing posts:

1. Go to **Admin → Feedback**
2. Find a post you're planning to work on
3. Change its status to **Planned**
4. It immediately appears on your public roadmap

That's it. [[Updating Statuses|Learn about updating statuses →]]

---

## Roadmap and the feedback tab

The roadmap and the feedback tab are two views of the same posts.

- **Feedback tab:** sorted by votes, shows all statuses
- **Roadmap tab:** shows only Planned, In Progress, Released -- organized by column

Users can switch between them freely. Both tabs are always visible unless your board is private.

---

## Sharing your roadmap

Your roadmap is always available at:

```
https://yoursubdomain.flowplanner.io/roadmap
```

Link directly to the roadmap when you want to show users what you're building -- in release announcements, social posts, or your marketing site.
