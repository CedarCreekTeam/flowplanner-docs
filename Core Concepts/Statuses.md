# Statuses

Statuses are how you move feedback through your workflow -- from raw submission to shipped feature. They're also what powers your roadmap: posts with Planned, In Progress, or Released status appear there automatically.

> [!quote] Status is your most important signal
> When you update a status, you're not just organizing -- you're ==communicating with every user who voted==.

## The five statuses

| Status | Meaning | Appears on roadmap? |
|--------|---------|---------------------|
| **Open** | New or under review -- no commitment yet | No |
| **Planned** | Committed to building -- coming soon | Yes |
| **In Progress** | Actively being worked on right now | Yes |
| **Released** | Shipped -- this is done | Yes |
| **Closed** | Won't build, duplicate, or out of scope | No |

---

## Open

The default status for all new posts. A post that's Open is in your inbox -- it's been acknowledged but no decision has been made yet. Most posts stay Open until you triage them.

> [!tip] Don't let Open pile up
> Review your Open posts regularly. Even if you're not building something, closing it with a note is better than leaving users wondering if anyone read it.

---

## Planned

Use Planned when you've made a real commitment to build something. This is a ==promise to your users== -- don't mark something Planned unless it's in your actual roadmap.

When you mark a post Planned, it:
- Moves to the Planned column on your public roadmap
- Is visible to everyone who visits your board

---

## In Progress

Mark a post In Progress when your team has actively started work. Users can see exactly what's being built right now.

> [!success] In Progress is powerful
> Users love seeing movement. A post that moves from Planned → In Progress shows the board is alive and decisions are being acted on.

---

## Released

Mark a post Released when the feature or fix has shipped to users. This is the ==most satisfying status update== -- and it closes the feedback loop.

When you mark a post Released, users who voted on it may be notified (depending on notification settings).

---

## Closed

Use Closed for posts you won't build. Always leave a comment explaining why -- users deserve to know if their idea isn't a fit. Good reasons to close a post:

- "This is out of scope for our current product direction."
- "This is a duplicate of [[link to original post]]."
- "We've decided to solve this differently -- see [Released post]."
