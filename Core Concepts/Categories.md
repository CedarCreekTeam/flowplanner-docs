# Categories

Categories let users classify their feedback when they submit it -- and let your team filter, sort, and triage posts by type. A well-chosen set of categories makes your board ==dramatically easier to manage==.

> [!quote] Categories are your first triage layer
> Before you even open a post, its category tells you what kind of response it needs and which team member should handle it.

## What categories do

- Users pick a category when submitting a post
- Posts can be filtered by category on the feedback board
- Your team can filter the admin view by category to work through one type at a time
- Categories help users discover related posts before submitting duplicates

---

## Creating categories

1. Go to **Admin → Categories**
2. Click **Add category**
3. Enter a name and choose a color
4. Save

Categories appear in the order you create them. Reorder them by dragging.

> [!tip] Good starting categories
> Keep it simple at first. Most boards start with:
> - **Feature Request** -- new functionality users want
> - **Bug** -- something that's broken
> - **Improvement** -- making existing features better
> - **Question** -- needs an answer, not a build

---

## How many categories should I have?

> [!note] Less is more
> If users have to think about which category to pick, you have too many. **5--7 categories** is the sweet spot for most boards. More than 10 and the filter bar becomes cluttered.

---

## Editing and deleting categories

- **Edit** a category name or color any time from **Admin → Categories**
- **Delete** removes the category from all future posts -- existing posts that used it are ==not affected== and keep their original category label

---

## Categories vs. statuses

These are easy to confuse:

| | Category | Status |
|---|----------|--------|
| **Set by** | User (on submit) | Admin (during triage) |
| **What it describes** | Type of feedback | Stage in your workflow |
| **Examples** | Feature Request, Bug | Open, Planned, In Progress |
